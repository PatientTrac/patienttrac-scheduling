HIPAA Compliance

PatientTrac Corp · PatientTracForge v0.3 · Last reviewed: April 24, 2026

PatientTracForge is built from the ground up to meet HIPAA's Privacy Rule, Security Rule, and Breach Notification Rule requirements. This page documents our technical and administrative safeguards, sub-processor BAAs, and how to request compliance documentation.

🛡
Business Associate
PatientTrac Corp operates as a HIPAA Business Associate. We sign a BAA with every Covered Entity customer before any PHI is processed.
🔐
Encryption
All PHI encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256). Database-level encryption via Supabase on AWS us-east-1.
🔑
Access Control
6-role RBAC matrix. TOTP MFA required for all admin and provider roles. Row Level Security isolates every tenant's data by org_id.
📋
Audit Logging
Every login, PHI access, and data modification is logged to saas.auth_audit_log. Retained for 6 years per HIPAA requirements.
🔔
Breach Notification
Breach notification to Covered Entities within 60 days of discovery, including details of PHI involved, individuals affected, and corrective actions.
🤝
Sub-processor BAAs
All sub-processors handling PHI (Supabase, Anthropic, Resend, Twilio) operate under executed Business Associate Agreements.
📄
Request a Business Associate Agreement
All Covered Entities and Business Associates using PatientTracForge must execute a BAA before storing Protected Health Information. Our BAA covers all sub-processors, data retention obligations, breach notification procedures, and PHI destruction upon termination.
Request BAA → legal@patienttrac.com

Administrative Safeguards (HIPAA § 164.308)

✓
Security Officer: Designated HIPAA Security Officer responsible for policy development, training, and incident response.
✓
Risk analysis: Annual HIPAA security risk assessment identifying threats to PHI confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
✓
Workforce training: All employees with PHI access complete HIPAA training at hire and annually. Background checks required.
✓
Access management: Least-privilege access. Access granted by role, reviewed quarterly. Terminated immediately upon employee departure.
✓
Incident response: Documented breach response procedure. Detection → containment → assessment → notification → remediation.
✓
Contingency plan: Automated Supabase backups (daily). Recovery Time Objective (RTO): 4 hours. Recovery Point Objective (RPO): 24 hours.
✓
Business associate management: BAAs executed with all sub-processors before PHI access. Annual review of sub-processor compliance.
✓
Evaluation: Annual technical and non-technical evaluation of HIPAA safeguards. Findings documented and remediated.

Physical Safeguards (HIPAA § 164.310)

PatientTracForge is fully cloud-hosted. Physical safeguards are implemented through our infrastructure providers:

✓
AWS (via Supabase): SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, HIPAA-eligible. Data centers with physical access controls, surveillance, and environmental safeguards. US-east-1 (Virginia).
✓
Netlify: CDN hosting with no PHI stored at CDN layer. All dynamic data transits to Supabase only. SOC 2 compliant.
✓
Workstation controls: Remote work policy requires encrypted drives, VPN access for administrative functions, and screen locks.
✓
Media disposal: Electronic PHI destroyed per NIST SP 800-88 standards upon contract termination. Certificate of destruction available on request.

Patient Rights & Provider Obligations

PatientTracForge enables Covered Entities to fulfill their patient rights obligations under HIPAA:

✓
Right of access: Providers can export a complete patient record (all encounters, diagnoses, billing) at any time via the patient profile page.
✓
Right to amend: Patient records can be updated or annotated at any time. All changes are timestamped and attributed to the modifying user.
✓
Right to accounting: The auth_audit_log and encounter_workflow tables provide a complete accounting of PHI disclosures and access events.
✓
Right to restrict: Providers can mark patient records for restricted access using the RBAC system. Records can be flagged to limit staff visibility.
✓
Right to confidential communications: Patient preferred contact method and language preferences are stored and honored in all communications.
